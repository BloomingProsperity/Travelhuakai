## Part 1: District-by-District Breakdown

### 1. Yuexiu District (越秀区)

**Cultural/historical character:** The oldest continuously inhabited core of Guangzhou and its political–cultural heart. Cantonese in every sense: the city was called Panyu here before it was Guangzhou. Layer upon layer of history — Nanyue Kingdom tombs, Ming dynasty walls, Republican-era civic architecture, post-1949 monuments.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** First-timers land here because the icon-density is unmatched: Five Rams Statue, Yuexiu Park, Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall, Nanyue King Mausoleum, Beijing Road's Tang-dynasty excavated road layers. All reachable on Metro Line 2.

**Top attractions:**
1. Yuexiu Park / Five Rams Statue (越秀公园 / 五羊雕像)
2. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (中山纪念堂)
3. Museum of the Western Han Dynasty Mausoleum of the Nanyue King (南越王博物院)

**Dim sum / food strength:** Strong. Lianxiang Lou (莲香楼) near Yuexiu Park is a century-old teahouse. Beijing Road also has street snack stalls and casual Cantonese eateries.

**Transport hub:** Not a rail terminus but very well served by Metro Lines 1, 2, and 6. Guangzhou Station (广州站, long-distance trains to northern China) is at the northwest edge of the district.

**Tier: MUST-VISIT.** The canonical first-day walk for any foreign tourist. Dense, walkable, historically rich, well-signposted in English at key monuments.

---

### 2. Liwan District (荔湾区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Old Guangzhou's trading and artisan quarter. Lingnan shophouse arcades (qilou 骑楼), antique markets, Cantonese opera roots, and — most visible to outsiders — Shamian Island, a former European and American concession from 1859 that was shaped by French and British colonial architecture.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Shamian Island is among the most photogenic 30 minutes of walking in any Chinese city. The Chen Clan Ancestral Hall delivers the best single explanation of Lingnan decorative craft anywhere. The Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street offers old Cantonese commercial atmosphere with snack shops.

**Top attractions:**
1. Shamian Island (沙面岛)
2. Chen Clan Ancestral Hall / Guangdong Folk Arts Museum (陈家祠 / 广东民间工艺博物馆)
3. Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街) + Guangzhou Restaurant (广州酒家)

**Dim sum / food strength:** Very strong — arguably the city's strongest. Tao Tao Ju (陶陶居, est. 1880, Michelin-listed), Guangzhou Restaurant (广州酒家, est. 1935), Panxi Restaurant (泮溪酒家, lakeside). These are the pilgrimage dim sum addresses for the whole Pearl River Delta.

**Transport hub:** Metro Lines 1 and 6 (Huangsha, Changshou Lu, Cultural Park stations). No intercity rail terminus.

**Tier: MUST-VISIT.** Essential for heritage tourism, the best dim sum address cluster in all of Guangdong, and the most foreigner-friendly traditional neighbourhood in the city.

---

### 3. Tianhe District (天河区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Guangzhou's financial and commercial engine since the 1990s. Home to Zhujiang New Town (珠江新城) CBD, Guangzhou's equivalent of Pudong: towers designed by Zaha Hadid (Opera House) and regional counterparts. Modern, international, polished.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** The Guangdong Museum (free), Guangzhou Opera House (stunning building, ticketed performances), Guangzhou K11 Art Mall, and the Pearl River night cruise departure points. Canton Tower is technically on the Haizhu bank directly across the river but defines the Tianhe skyline. Zhujiang Park offers greenery in the CBD.

**Top attractions:**
1. Guangdong Museum (广东省博物馆) — free, Zhujiang New Town
2. Guangzhou Opera House (广州大剧院) — exterior landmark, interior by ticket
3. Zhujiang New Town promenade / Huacheng Square (花城广场)

**Dim sum / food strength:** Moderate for tradition; high for quality and comfort. Dian Dou De (点都德) chain has multiple branches here. Modern Cantonese fine dining is strong. Less atmospheric than Liwan.

**Transport hub:** Metro Lines 3, 5, APM people-mover. Guangzhou East Station (广州东站, intercity trains to Shenzhen and some intercity routes) is in this district.

**Tier: MUST-VISIT** for modern Guangzhou, museum-goers, opera-lovers, and anyone wanting an evening Pearl River promenade.

---

### 4. Haizhu District (海珠区)

**Cultural/historical character:** The large island formed by the Pearl River's two channels. Mixed: older southern residential areas, the Canton Fair Pazhou Complex, the growing Haizhu Wetland, and creative industries.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Canton Tower is on the northern tip of Haizhu (No. 222 West Yuejiang Road). The Pearl River night cruise Tianzi Pier is near here. Haizhu National Wetland Park is a rare urban nature reserve. The Canton Fair Complex at Pazhou Island draws over 200,000 trade buyers per session — leisure tourists usually avoid Pazhou during fair weeks.

**Top attractions:**
1. Canton Tower (广州塔) — northern tip of Haizhu
2. Haizhu National Wetland Park (海珠国家湿地公园)
3. Pazhou / Canton Fair Complex (琶洲展览馆) — when fair is not running, quiet riverside walks

**Dim sum / food strength:** Moderate. Several local Cantonese restaurants but not a destination for dim sum pilgrimage.

**Transport hub:** Metro Lines 2, 8. Pazhou served by Metro Line 8 (Pazhou Station). No intercity rail terminus.

**Tier: MUST-VISIT** for Canton Tower and Pearl River ambience. The wetland park earns it for nature-seekers.

---

### 5. Baiyun District (白云区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Named for Baiyun (White Cloud) Mountain, which rises at the district's eastern edge. A mix of urban sprawl, the Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN), and the mountain scenic area — a National 5A-level attraction with more than 20 million visitors annually.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Baiyun Mountain is Guangzhou's most-visited day-trip destination: 382 m peak, cable car, bird sanctuaries, botanical garden, and sweeping city views. Visitors arriving or departing from the new Terminal 3 (opened October 30, 2025) pass through this district.

**Top attractions:**
1. Baiyun Mountain Scenic Area (白云山风景区) — National 5A
2. Mingchun Valley bird sanctuary (鸣春谷)
3. Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport T3 (opened 2025-10-30, see Pitfalls section)

**Dim sum / food strength:** Low-to-moderate. Not a restaurant destination; eat before coming.

**Transport hub:** Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN). Metro Line 3 connects T1/T2 to city; T3 requires transfer to shuttle bus from Gaozeng Station or use of Guangdong Intercity Railway (Baiyun Airport East Station directly connected to T3).

**Tier: WORTH-IT** for Baiyun Mountain. International arrivals/departures cannot avoid the airport.

---

### 6. Huangpu District (黄埔区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Guangzhou's innovation and tech-industrial belt (Guangzhou Science City). Also houses important history: Changzhou Island has the Whampoa Military Academy site, the birthplace of modern Chinese military leadership in the 1920s, and Huangpu Ancient Port, a maritime Silk Road node dating to the Sui/Tang dynasties.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** The Whampoa Military Academy Memorial Hall is free and historically riveting for anyone interested in 20th-century Chinese history (Sun Yat-sen, Chiang Kai-shek, Zhou Enlai all connected here). Huangpu Ancient Port Museum covers the pre-colonial maritime trade story.

**Top attractions:**
1. Whampoa Military Academy Memorial Hall (黄埔军校旧址纪念馆) — free, closed Mondays
2. Huangpu Ancient Port (黄埔古港)
3. Huangpu Ancient Village Museum

**Dim sum / food strength:** Low. Industrial/tech zone; eat downtown.

**Transport hub:** Metro Line 5 serves the inner part; Line 6 extension and new Tram Line 2 (opened mid-2025) serve Science City. No intercity terminal.

**Tier: WORTH-IT** for history enthusiasts willing to make the half-day trip. Skip on a short first trip.

---

### 7. Panyu District (番禺区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Once a separate municipality, now Guangzhou's southern residential and leisure district. Contains Guangzhou South Station, the Chimelong resort complex, Shawan Ancient Town (800-year Lingnan heritage), and university town (Xiaoguwei Island).

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Chimelong is one of Asia's top theme-park complexes (Safari Park, Paradise amusement park, Water Park) and draws families and thrill-seekers. Shawan Ancient Town offers a low-key, low-cost alternative to Liwan's more crowded streets. Lingnan Impression Park packages intangible cultural heritage shows in a scenic setting.

**Top attractions:**
1. Chimelong Tourist Resort (长隆旅游度假区) — Safari Park + Paradise + Water Park
2. Shawan Ancient Town (沙湾古镇) — free entry, charges only for special exhibitions
3. Lingnan Impression Park (岭南印象园) — 09:30–18:00

**Dim sum / food strength:** Moderate. Shawan has its own Lingnan snacks (ginger milk curd). Chimelong resort has food courts for all budgets.

**Transport hub:** Guangzhou South Station (广州南站) — the city's main high-speed rail terminus, connecting to Hong Kong (47 min), Shenzhen (30 min), Beijing (8 h), Shanghai (5 h). Metro Lines 2 and 7 serve the district; Lines 3 and 7 serve Chimelong via Hanxi Changlong Station.

**Tier: MUST-VISIT** for families (Chimelong). Worth-it for history fans (Shawan). Guangzhou South Station is the logistics nexus for all HSR departures.

---

### 8. Huadu District (花都区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Historically a leather and auto-manufacturing zone; now home to Guangzhou Sunac Cultural Tourism City and wholesale markets. North of the city centre, 30+ km from Tianhe.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Largely a non-destination for leisure tourists. Some wholesale buyers visit the leather and garment markets.

**Top attractions:**
1. Guangzhou Sunac Cultural Tourism City (广州融创文旅城)
2. Huadu leather and fur wholesale markets (wholesale buyers only)

**Dim sum / food strength:** Low.

**Transport hub:** Adjacent to Baiyun Airport (CAN) on the north; Metro Line 9 connects Huadu to downtown.

**Tier: SKIP ON FIRST TRIP.** Nothing here competes with the central or Panyu districts for typical tourist itineraries.

---

### 9. Nansha District (南沙区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Guangzhou's Free Trade Zone and its only coastal area (Pearl River Estuary). New city development, Nansha Ferry Port, and heritage/nature sites including the Tianhou (Mazu) Temple and Nansha Wetland.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** The Mazu/Tianhou Palace is the largest Mazu temple in Southeast Asia and commands a dramatic hilltop site. The Nansha "Lovers' Road" 20 km coastal trail opened in late 2025. Ferries to Hong Kong (Nansha Ferry Terminal) and Macau run from here.

**Top attractions:**
1. Nansha Tianhou Palace / Mazu Temple (天后宫)
2. Nansha Wetland Park (南沙湿地公园) — migratory bird reserve
3. Nansha Ferry Terminal — ferries to Hong Kong and Zhongshan

**Dim sum / food strength:** Low in tourist terms; local seafood is the draw.

**Transport hub:** Metro Lines 4 and 18 serve Nansha; Line 18 connects fast to Tianhe CBD. Nansha Ferry Port links Hong Kong (~1 h water journey).

**Tier: WORTH-IT** for the Mazu temple, ferry connections, and anyone who wants coastal Guangdong without Shenzhen crowds. Skip on a 2–3 day itinerary.

---

### 10. Zengcheng District (增城区)

**Cultural/historical character:** East Guangzhou. Agricultural, rural-suburban. Famous for Zengcheng Sijiao glutinous rice, Baima Mountain white water rafting, and traditional Hakka villages. About 60 km from the city centre.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Day-trip for outdoor activities (white water rafting in summer), Hakka heritage villages, and relatively unspoilt Lingnan countryside. Not a first-trip priority.

**Top attractions:**
1. Pai Pai Gorge white-water rafting (白水寨瀑布)
2. Zengcheng ancient Hakka villages
3. Baima Mountain Scenic Area

**Dim sum / food strength:** Low for tourist-grade dim sum.

**Transport hub:** Metro Line 13 (Zengcheng Line) connects downtown. No intercity terminus.

**Tier: SKIP ON FIRST TRIP.** Worth it for outdoor enthusiasts or Hakka culture specialists on extended stays.

---

### 11. Conghua District (从化区)

**Cultural/historical character:** Guangzhou's northernmost district, 75 km from the city centre. Renowned for rare bicarbonate-rich hot springs (Wenquan Town), Liuxi River valley, and Shimen National Forest Park.

**Why a foreign tourist visits:** Conghua hot springs are the most accessible premium hot-spring experience near Guangzhou. Xianmuyuan Hot Spring Park and Bishuiwan Hot Spring Resort are the headline facilities. Nature walks and cooler temperatures attract weekenders fleeing the urban heat.

**Top attractions:**
1. Xianmuyuan Hot Spring Park (仙沐园温泉)
2. Bishuiwan Hot Spring Resort (碧水湾温泉度假村)
3. Shimen National Forest Park (石门国家森林公园) — spring flower valleys

**Dim sum / food strength:** Low. Resort food; eat in the city before or after.

**Transport hub:** Metro Line 14 connects Conghua to downtown. No intercity terminus.

**Tier: SKIP ON FIRST TRIP** for sightseeing; WORTH-IT for multi-day stays seeking spa/nature escape.

## Part 2: Top 14 Attractions for Foreign Tourists

---

### 1. Yuexiu Park / Five Rams Statue (越秀公园 / 五羊雕像)

**District:** Yuexiu
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** The Five Rams Statue (1959, 11 m tall, carved from 130+ pieces of granite) is Guangzhou's civic symbol, rooted in the legend of five celestial rams who brought rice seeds to bless the city. Yuexiu Park is Guangzhou's largest urban park (860,000 m²) and also contains Zhenhai Tower (Guangzhou Museum, a Ming-dynasty watchtower) and the remains of the ancient city wall. Packed with locals from 6 AM doing tai chi; best light for photography is morning.

**Booking method:** No booking required. Walk-in, free. Zhenhai Tower interior requires a separate ticket (10 RMB).

**Daily limit / crowd:** No cap on the park; busy on weekends and public holidays. Zhenhai Tower can queue 20–30 min on peak days.

**Opening hours:** 06:00–22:00 daily (park). Zhenhai Tower 09:00–17:00, closed Mondays.

**Foreign card acceptance:** No card needed — entry is free. Zhenhai Tower ticket windows accept cash (RMB); some accept WeChat/Alipay.

**English signage:** Bilingual (Chinese/English) at major landmarks including the statue and Zhenhai Tower.

**Official URL:** http://www.gz.gov.cn/guangzhouinternational/visitors/whattosee/

**Image:** https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/42/Guangzhou_Opera_House_and_Canton_Tower.JPG (CC0 Public Domain, by Ecow, 2012)

### 2. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (中山纪念堂)

**District:** Yuexiu
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** Completed in 1931 in tribute to the "Father of Modern China," this octagonal hall seats 4,700 and merges traditional Chinese palatial architecture with early-20th-century engineering. The blue-glazed roof tiles, vast column-free interior, and the story of Sun Yat-sen's three decades of revolutionary activity based in Guangzhou make this essential for visitors interested in modern Chinese history. Free guided tours at 10 AM and 3 PM daily.

**Booking method:** Online or on-site. Passport holders can exchange their voucher at the ticket window. Address: No. 259 Dongfeng Middle Road, Yuexiu District. Metro: Line 2 to Memorial Hall Station.

**Daily limit / crowd:** Moderate. Occasional large performances sell out the hall itself but the grounds stay open.

**Opening hours:** 09:00–17:30 daily (last entry 17:00).

**Admission:** 10 RMB adults; free for seniors 65+; half-price for ages 60–64 and undergraduate students.

**Foreign card acceptance:** Cash/WeChat preferred at ticket window; no universal foreign card acceptance confirmed.

**English signage:** Good English labelling throughout. Free guided tours at set times; multilingual paid tours available.

**Official URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen_Memorial_Hall_(Guangzhou)

---

### 3. Museum of the Western Han Dynasty Mausoleum of the Nanyue King (南越王博物院)

**District:** Yuexiu
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** In 1983 construction workers discovered the intact 2,100-year-old tomb of Zhao Mo, the second king of the Nanyue Kingdom (est. 203 BCE). The tomb yielded 1,000+ artefacts including the only jade burial suit found in the Lingnan region, rhinoceros horn cups, and bronze musical instruments — all displayed in situ. Among South China's most significant archaeological museums.

**Booking method:** WeChat reservation via official account "南越王博物院" (nanyuewangbowuyuan). Real-name reservation required. Two sites: Site of King's Tomb at No. 867 Jiefang North Road; Site of Palace and Garden at No. 316 Zhongshan 4th Road.

**Daily limit / crowd:** Timed-entry via WeChat reservation. Site of King's Tomb: 10 RMB full / 5 RMB concession. Site of Palace and Garden: free.

**Opening hours:** Tuesday–Sunday 09:00–17:30 (ticket cut-off 17:00); closed Mondays except public holidays.

**Foreign card acceptance:** Ticket purchased via WeChat mini-program; international visitors can link an international card to Alipay or pay cash on-site.

**English signage:** Moderate. Brief English exhibit labels; audio guides available for hire. Tel: 020-36182920 (King's Tomb).

**Official URL:** https://www.nywmuseum.org.cn/En/News/Visit

---

### 4. Chen Clan Ancestral Hall (陈家祠)

**District:** Liwan (No. 34 Enlongli, Zhongshan 7th Road)
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** Built 1888–1894 by 72 Chen-surname clans as an exam-preparation hostel and worship site, this compound is the supreme surviving example of Lingnan decorative architecture. Every surface — ridge beams, screen walls, cornices, courtyards — is covered with polychrome ceramic figurines, wood-carved scenes from classical novels, stone bas-reliefs, brick carvings, and iron castings. Now the Guangdong Folk Arts Museum. Called "the Pearl of Lingnan Architecture."

**Booking method:** Walk-in or online ticketing (Trip.com, Klook). Passport accepted at ticket window. English guided tours on-site: 260–400 RMB for groups up to 5.

**Daily limit / crowd:** No daily limit reported but very crowded on weekends. Arrive before 10 AM.

**Opening hours:** Daily 09:00–17:30, ticket cut-off 17:00; closed Tuesdays.

**Admission:** 10 RMB general; student/child/senior discounts with valid ID.

**Foreign card acceptance:** Cash or mobile payment preferred.

**English signage:** English labels at major panels; guided tours in English available for hire.

**Official URL:** http://www.gz.gov.cn/guangzhouinternational/visitors/ (Guangzhou government tourism portal; no standalone English site for the hall)

**Image:** https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e2/Chan_Clan_Ancestral_Hall.JPG (CC BY-SA 3.0, by Lai.jack, 2013)

### 5. Shamian Island (沙面岛)

**District:** Liwan
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** A 0.3 km² sand-bar in the Pearl River that served as the British and French concession from 1859 to 1943. The island retains over 150 European-style buildings — consulates, banks, churches, trading-house villas — shaded by 200-year-old banyan trees along broad, quiet avenues. No cars, no hawkers inside the island, no admission charge. Often described as the calmest 90 minutes in Guangzhou. The White Swan Hotel (reopened 2015) occupies the south bank and is fully English-service.

**Booking method:** None required. Free public space. Walk across the bridges from Huangsha Metro Station (Line 1 or 6).

**Daily limit / crowd:** No limit. Peak on weekend afternoons; early morning is peaceful.

**Opening hours:** 24 hours (public space).

**Foreign card acceptance:** Not applicable — public street area. Cafes and restaurants inside accept cards variably.

**English signage:** Excellent. Historical plaques in English throughout.

**Official URL:** http://www.gz.gov.cn/guangzhouinternational/

**Note on scam:** See Part 5 (Pitfall 5.2) — tea house/art exhibition scam specifically targets foreign tourists at Shamian Island. Do not accept invitations from strangers.

### 6. Canton Tower (广州塔)

**District:** Haizhu (No. 222 West Yuejiang Road)
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** At 600 m (450 m structure + 150 m antenna mast), Canton Tower is the tallest TV/observation tower in China and third tallest in the world. The twisted-waist design is one of Guangzhou's most recognizable silhouettes. Indoor and outdoor observation decks give 360° views of the CBD, Pearl River, and surrounding districts. The glass sky corridor and bubble tram (revolving around the outside) offer additional thrills. Best visited at dusk for the transition from daylight to city-lights.

**Booking method:** Online at cantontower.com or trip.com, or at the West Ticket Office on the day with passport + QR voucher.

**Daily limit / crowd:** Capped by capacity; sell out on public holidays. Book online 1–3 days ahead for peak periods.

**Opening hours:** 09:30–22:30 (last check-in 22:00).

**Admission:** 150 RMB base (observation decks); 398 RMB for full experience including bubble tram and sky walk.

**Foreign card acceptance:** Online booking via international card (Visa/Mastercard) supported. On-site: cash/WeChat/Alipay.

**English signage:** Comprehensive. English-speaking visitor centre staff 09:30–22:00.

**Official URL:** https://www.cantontower.com/en/

**Image:** https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Guangzhou_Tower.jpg/500px-Guangzhou_Tower.jpg (CC BY-SA 3.0)

### 7. Pearl River Night Cruise (珠江夜游)

**District:** Haizhu/Tianhe (departs from Tianzi Pier or Haixinsha West Pier)
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** The Pearl River illuminated-skyline cruise is Guangzhou's signature evening activity. The 60–90 minute route passes below Canton Tower, along Zhujiang New Town's glittering towers, past the colonial Shamian shores, and through bridges lit in colour. The 8 PM departure catches the full LED show of Canton Tower.

**Booking method:** Online (cantontower.com/en/pearlriver, Trip.com, Klook) or walk-up at Tianzi Pier. Recommended to book in advance on weekend evenings.

**Daily limit / crowd:** Boats run from approximately 19:00–22:00 with multiple departures. Tianzi Pier can have queues; Haixinsha West Pier is less crowded.

**Opening hours:** Evening sailings approximately 19:00–22:00.

**Admission:** 60–80 RMB standard; 150–300 RMB luxury dinner cruise.

**Foreign card acceptance:** Online platforms (Klook, Trip.com) accept international cards. Walk-up: cash/WeChat/Alipay.

**English signage:** Onboard announcements in Mandarin and English on main tourist boats.

**Official URL:** https://www.cantontower.com/en/pearlriver/otherships/

### 8. Guangdong Museum (广东省博物馆)

**District:** Tianhe (No. 2 Zhujiang East Road, Zhujiang New Town)
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** Opened 2010 in a striking bronze lacquer-box building by Rocco Design. Houses 160,000+ objects across three major wings: Guangdong History and Culture, Natural History, and Art and Crafts (Chaozhou woodcarving, Duan inkstones, ceramics). Free admission makes it Guangzhou's most accessible large museum. The building itself — a massive cube apparently suspended above its plinth — is an architectural draw.

**Booking method:** WeChat official account "广东省博物馆" for advance reservation (recommended on weekends). Foreign tourists can present passport at the West Gate service desk for a free on-the-spot ticket without online booking.

**Daily limit / crowd:** Visitor numbers are controlled. Weekends and public holidays fill capacity by 10 AM. Tuesday–Thursday mornings are quietest.

**Opening hours:** Tuesday–Sunday 09:00–17:00 (last entry 16:00); closed Mondays.

**Admission:** Free.

**Foreign card acceptance:** No payment required — free.

**English signage:** Bilingual throughout major exhibition halls.

**Official URL:** https://www.gdmuseum.com (English section noted in Wikidata record https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q5613636; direct English subdomain was unreachable at research time)

### 9. Baiyun Mountain Scenic Area (白云山风景区)

**District:** Baiyun
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** A National 5A-level scenic area 12 km from the city centre. The Moxing Ridge peak (382 m) gives panoramic views over Greater Guangzhou on clear days. The 21 km² park contains a cable car, Mingchun Valley giant bird sanctuary (50,000 m² open-air cage; 5,000+ birds, 150+ species), Yunxi Botanical Garden (1,320 species), and the Guangzhou Sculpture Park. More than 20 million visitors per year. Popular for early-morning Cantonese exercise culture.

**Booking method:** Walk-in, buy at entrance gate. No advance reservation required.

**Daily limit / crowd:** No daily limit cap but extremely busy on weekends (arrive before 8 AM or after 4 PM). Cable car queues can reach 45 min on peak days.

**Opening hours:** 06:00–21:00 (park); cable car approximately 09:00–18:00 (varies by season).

**Admission:** 5 RMB park entrance. Cable car separate charge (approximately 30–50 RMB one-way). Mingchun Valley separate (approximately 35–45 RMB).

**Foreign card acceptance:** Cash strongly preferred at park gates. Foreign cards rarely accepted. Bring RMB or use Alipay linked to an international card.

**English signage:** Moderate at key trail junctions; limited at smaller gardens.

**Official URL:** http://www.baiyunshan.com.cn (Chinese only); English context: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baiyun_Mountain_(Guangdong)

### 10. Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street (上下九步行街)

**District:** Liwan
**Tier:** Must-visit

**Why visit:** Guangzhou's first formal commercial pedestrian street (opened September 1995), stretching 1,200 m through arcaded Lingnan qilou shophouses. Over 300 shops selling clothing, snacks, and local goods, anchored by the Guangzhou Restaurant (est. 1935) at the Wenchang Road end. Less expensive and more atmospheric than Beijing Road. Evening atmosphere is Cantonese street food at its most accessible.

**Booking method:** Public street — no booking needed. Guangzhou Restaurant takes walk-ins; expect queues for yum cha 07:30–10:30 AM on weekends.

**Opening hours:** Shops generally 09:00–22:00; restaurants may open from 07:00.

**Admission:** Free (public pedestrian street).

**Foreign card acceptance:** Mix. Major chain stores accept UnionPay; WeChat/Alipay preferred; limited international card acceptance.

**English signage:** Street directional signs in English. Most individual shops: Chinese only.

**Official URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shangxiajiu_Pedestrian_Street

### 11. Beijing Road Pedestrian Street (北京路步行街)

**District:** Yuexiu
**Tier:** Worth-it

**Why visit:** Yuexiu's commercial high street, 1,500 m long. Its distinctive feature is transparent floor panels revealing excavated Tang Dynasty (618–907 AD), Song Dynasty, and Ming Dynasty road layers directly beneath visitors' feet — a literal stratigraphy of Guangzhou's commercial continuity. Grandview Mall and The 7th Heaven Plaza anchor modern retail. Best combined with a Yuexiu Park visit.

**Booking method:** None required. Public pedestrian street.

**Opening hours:** Most shops 10:00–22:00.

**Admission:** Free.

**Foreign card acceptance:** Major malls (Grandview) accept Visa/Mastercard. Street stalls: cash/WeChat only.

**English signage:** Moderate. Tang road excavation site has English interpretation panels.

**Official URL:** https://www.travelchinaguide.com/cityguides/guangdong/guangzhou/beijing-road.htm

### 12. Chimelong Tourist Resort (长隆旅游度假区)

**District:** Panyu
**Tier:** Must-visit (for families / theme-park visitors)

**Why visit:** Asia's largest theme-park resort complex, comprising: Chimelong Safari Park (world's largest wildlife theme park, 500+ species, 20,000 animals, 10 giant pandas, drive-through safari zones), Chimelong Paradise (amusement park, 10-loop roller coaster, approximately 60 rides), and Chimelong Water Park (world's largest water park by Guinness certified attendance, 70+ rides). Hotels on-site. Metro Lines 3 or 7 to Hanxi Changlong Station, exit D/E, then free shuttle.

**Booking method:** Online (Trip.com, Klook, chimelong.com) or on-site. Book 1–3 days ahead on weekends/holidays. Passport or ID used for real-name ticket.

**Daily limit / crowd:** Daily capacity limits apply. School holidays: extremely crowded. Weekday visit strongly recommended.

### 13. Haizhu National Wetland Park (海珠国家湿地公园)

**District:** Haizhu (No. 168 Xinjiaozhong Road)
**Tier:** Worth-it

**Why visit:** The largest urban wetland park in China (1,100 hectares), formed from the historic fruit orchards (lychee, longan, starfruit) that once covered Haizhu Island. The wetland is a flyway stop for migratory birds; birdwatchers report 100+ species seasonally. A rare chance to see traditional Guangdong fruit-orchard ecology surviving inside a megalopolis. ## Part 3: Yum Cha / Cantonese Food Districts

### The Yum Cha Rhythm

Cantonese dim sum culture (飲茶, yum cha — "drink tea") follows a distinct daily rhythm:

- **Early birds 06:00–08:30:** Elderly locals occupy window tables; limited selection but freshest tea.
- **Prime session 08:30–11:30:** Full trolley (older venues) or order-sheet selection. Hargow (蝦餃), siu mai (燒賣), char siu bao (叉燒包), cheung fun (腸粉), egg tarts (蛋撻).
- **Peak 10:00–12:00:** Weekend queues of 30–60 minutes at famous venues.
- **Lunch dim sum 11:30–14:30:** Continues in most restaurants.
- **Afternoon tea 14:00–17:00:** Lighter selection; cakes and congee.
- **Evening dim sum 18:00+:** Many restaurants continue; late-night dim sum available at Tao Tao Ju until approximately 23:00.

**Tea seat fee (茶位費 / cha wei fei):** Every diner pays 5–20 RMB per person at the start, covering unlimited hot water for the tea you select (typically pu'er, chrysanthemum, jasmine, or longjing). This is a legitimate, centuries-old Cantonese custom — not a scam. Draft Guangdong regulations (2024) require restaurants to display tea selection and prices clearly. The fee is not negotiable and not a trap.

---

### District 1: Liwan — The Pilgrimage Quarter

The undisputed heartland of serious yum cha for both locals and food tourists.

**Tao Tao Ju (陶陶居)** — Est. 1880. Dishifu Road (地十甫路), Liwan. Michelin-listed. Multi-floor; ground floor uses traditional trolley service. Photo menu available. Opens 07:00; dim sum available 08:00–23:00 (multiple rounds including late-night). Approximately 50–80 RMB per head. Foreigner-friendly: High. Source: https://guide.michelin.com/us/en/guangdong-province/guangzhou/restaurant/tao-tao-ju-ya-yuan

**Guangzhou Restaurant (广州酒家)** — Est. 1935. Wenchang South Road junction with Shangxiajiu. Described locally as "No.1 place to eat in Guangzhou." No English menu; heavily local; arrive before 08:00 for a seat on weekends. Approximately 50–100 RMB per head. Foreigner-friendly: Moderate (no English service; picture-pointing works).

**Panxi Restaurant (泮溪酒家)** — Liwan Lake Park. Lakeside garden setting. Mid-range. Cantonese banquet and dim sum. Less crowded than the two above. Foreigner-friendly: Moderate.

---

### District 2: Yuexiu — Historic Centre Options

**Lianxiang Lou (莲香楼)** — Near Yuexiu Park. Est. 1889. Famous for cocktail buns and wife cakes. Limited English but manageable with photos. Opens 06:00. Approximately 30–60 RMB per head.

**Beijing Road vicinity:** Multiple contemporary dim sum chains including Dian Dou De (点都德) branches. Picture menus, WeChat ordering. Foreigner-friendly: Very high.

---

### District 3: Tianhe — Modern-Comfort Options

**Dian Dou De (点都德)** — Multiple branches in Tianhe and across the city. Modern chain with photo menus and some branches offering English digital ordering. Approximately 100–150 RMB per head. Best for first-time dim sum visitors nervous about language. Foreigner-friendly: Very high.

**Hongtu Hall (鸿图厅)** — Michelin-starred; upscale. Approximately 300+ RMB per head for dim sum service.

---

### District 4: Haizhu / Shamian — Upscale Waterfront

**White Swan Hotel dim sum (Shamian Island)** — River views, English menus, approximately 200–400 RMB per head. Michelin-cited. Most foreigner-accessible formal dim sum experience in Guangzhou but at premium prices. Advance reservation recommended.

---

### Cantonese Roast Meats (燒味) and Street Eating

Char siu (叉燒, BBQ pork), roast goose (燒鵝), and soy-sauce chicken (豉油雞) are available at dedicated siu mei shops throughout Liwan and Yuexiu. Look for shops with hanging meats in the window; point and they will chop a portion. Approximately 30–80 RMB per portion.

**Bingsheng Pinwei (炳胜品味)** — Multiple locations; known for roast meats and live seafood. Mid-to-high range.

### Cantonese Dessert Shops (糖水 / Tong Sui)

**Nanxin Milk Dessert (南信牛奶甜品专家)** — Dishifu Road, Liwan. Est. 1934. Famous for double-skin milk (双皮奶) and ginger milk curd (姜撞奶). Approximately 15–25 RMB per bowl. Foreigner-friendly: High (glass-case display; pointing works).

**Ordering reality for foreigners:** Most traditional dim sum restaurants and dessert shops do not have English menus. Staff are unlikely to speak English. Strategies: check what other tables are eating and point; use a translation app to photograph the menu; bring a WeChat contact who can type in Chinese on your behalf. Modern chains (Dian Dou De) use QR-code menus with photos and some have English.

## Part 4: Canton Fair Impact (April / October Bi-annually)

### What Is the Canton Fair

The China Import and Export Fair (中国进出口商品交易会), universally called the Canton Fair, is the world's oldest and largest trade fair, held bi-annually since 1957. The venue is the Pazhou Complex (琶洲展览馆) on Pazhou Island, Haizhu District — 1.55 million m² making it Asia's largest exhibition centre.

**Official site:** https://www.cantonfair.org.cn/en-US

### 2025–2026 Schedule

| Edition | Dates | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|---------|
| 137th Spring 2025 | Apr 15 – May 5, 2025 | Apr 15–19 | Apr 23–27 | May 1–5 |
| 138th Autumn 2025 | Oct 15 – Nov 4, 2025 | Oct 15–19 | Oct 23–27 | Oct 31–Nov 4 |
| 139th Spring 2026 | Apr 15 – May 5, 2026 | Apr 15–19 | Apr 23–27 | May 1–5 |

Each phase covers different product categories: Phase 1 electronics/industrial; Phase 2 consumer goods/gifts/home; Phase 3 textiles/apparel/food. Visiting hours: 09:30–18:00.

### What Changes for Ordinary Tourists During Fair Weeks

**Hotels:** Prices spike 50–300% across the entire city, not just Pazhou. Budget options near Pazhou go from approximately 300 RMB/night to 900+ RMB/night. The Westin Pazhou and Langham Place reach USD 250–300 per night. Rooms sell out across Tianhe and Yuexiu. Book 4–6 months ahead if visiting during April or October.

**Transport:** Metro Line 8 to Pazhou Station carries approximately 92,000 extra passengers per day during fair sessions. Expect packed trains morning and evening. Consider adjusting sightseeing to avoid peak commute hours (08:00–10:00, 17:00–19:30).

**Restaurants near Pazhou/Haizhu:** Fully booked; prices elevated. Avoid eating near the exhibition centre if you are not a fair visitor; go to Liwan or Tianhe instead.

**Taxis and ride-hail:** Surcharges apply in the Pazhou/Haizhu area. DiDi is more reliable than street taxis but expect waits.

**Recommendation for leisure tourists:** If possible, avoid Guangzhou entirely during Canton Fair weeks in April and October, or book accommodation at least 4 months ahead and budget 2x normal hotel costs.

### Who Can Enter the Canton Fair

The fair is for registered trade buyers. Foreign passport holders can register at cantonfair.org.cn for free entry as an overseas buyer. Casual tourists cannot walk in without a buyer badge.

## Part 5: Practical Pitfalls Specific to Guangzhou

### 5.1 Cha Wei (茶位費) — Normal Restaurant Charge, Not a Scam

Every diner in a traditional Cantonese teahouse or yum cha restaurant is charged a tea seat fee of approximately 5–20 RMB per person. This covers unlimited hot water and the tea you select (pu'er is standard; chrysanthemum, jasmine, and green tea are common alternatives). The practice dates to Qing-dynasty teahouses and is legally regulated in Guangdong under 2024 draft standards requiring clear price display and a selection of teas. If you see this charge on your bill, it is normal and expected.

**Source:** https://www.citynewsservice.cn/articles/tea-fee-sparks-stir-price-of-a-seat-in-guangdongs-tea-culture-bmebar6m

---

### 5.2 Tea House / Art Exhibition Scam on Shamian Island

This is Guangzhou's most-reported tourist scam and operates specifically near Shamian Island. The mechanics: a young, friendly, fluent-English-speaking stranger — often claiming to be a student from Sun Yat-sen University or Guangdong University of Foreign Studies — approaches foreign tourists and invites them to a "traditional Cantonese tea ceremony" or a "student art exhibition" nearby. The venue looks authentic. After consuming tea and viewing art, a bill for 2,500–7,000 RMB per person is presented. When the victim protests, a group of men appears. Payment is coerced.

**Rule:** Do not accept any invitation from a stranger at Shamian Island for tea, food, art, or cultural experiences. This applies regardless of how friendly, genuine, or culturally framed the approach is. If you want a tea ceremony, book through your hotel concierge with a fixed, published price.

**Source:** https://tabiji.ai/scams/guangzhou/ ; https://beforeyougotravels.com/destinations/guangzhou

---

### 5.3 Black Taxis (黑车) at Guangzhou South Station

Guangzhou South Station (广州南站) — the high-speed rail terminus in Panyu — has a persistent population of unlicensed private-car drivers who approach foreign passengers inside the station and immediately outside the exits. These drivers quote flat rates that sound reasonable but are typically 3–5x the metered taxi fare.

**Mitigations:**
- Use the official metered taxi rank (green/yellow licensed taxis with meters at designated stands on the ground level exit).
- Use Metro Line 2 or Line 7 from Guangzhou South to the city centre (4–8 RMB, approximately 20–40 min to Tianhe).
- Use DiDi (Chinese ride-hail app) from the designated DiDi pickup area on the B1 level — far safer than street taxis.
- Never follow anyone who approaches you unprompted inside or immediately outside the station.

**Source:** https://travelchina.tips/blogs/practical-guides/guangzhou-transport-hsr-guide-foreigners

---

### 5.4 Baiyun Airport Terminal 3 — Apps Showing Outdated Information (Critical Alert)

Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN) opened Terminal 3 (T3) on October 30, 2025. T3 is the new flagship international terminal: 422,000 m² flower-shaped design, biometric boarding, China's first open-air airport observation deck, and direct Guangdong Intercity Railway connection.

**The pitfall:** As of early 2026, some third-party apps (hotel concierge booking tools, older versions of map applications, some airline booking systems) continue to show all Guangzhou departures as Terminal 1 or Terminal 2. Foreign travellers may go to the wrong terminal.

**What to do:**
- Check your airline's check-in confirmation email for the terminal number.
- Check the official Guangzhou Baiyun Airport website: http://www.baiyunairport.com
- If uncertain: A free 24-hour inter-terminal shuttle bus runs between T1 (Gate 12), T2 (Gate 42), and T3 (Gate 62) every 10–30 minutes. An intercity train also runs every 17 minutes between terminals.
- From T3 to city: Metro Line 3 to Gaozeng Station then T3 shuttle bus (every 15 min); or Guangdong Intercity Railway from Baiyun Airport East Station (directly connected to T3 Transportation Center).
- E-hailing/DiDi pickups at T3 are in Parking Lot P12.

**Source:** http://www.eguangzhou.gov.cn/gzservice/content/post_39620.html (Guangzhou City Government official English portal, 2025)

---

### 5.5 Payment Reality for Foreign Cards

Guangzhou is more cash-dependent than Beijing or Shanghai for small transactions:

- **Metro:** Accepts cash, Guangzhou Transportation Smart Card, Alipay, or WeChat Pay at gates. International credit cards are beginning to be accepted at some gates from 2026 but coverage is inconsistent — do not rely on it.
- **Restaurants / street food:** Cash (RMB) or WeChat/Alipay. Visa/Mastercard accepted mainly at international hotel restaurants and major mall food courts.
- **Alipay International (Tour Pass):** Link a Visa/Mastercard to Alipay's "Tour Pass" feature; load a fixed amount (100/500/1,000 RMB) and spend like a local. Single transaction limit is USD 5,000 equivalent.
- **WeChat Pay:** Can also be linked to international cards for small transactions.
- **Practical recommendation:** Set up Alipay before arrival. Carry 500 RMB in cash (100-yuan notes) for emergencies. Inspect any 100 RMB notes received in change — counterfeit notes exist.

**Source:** https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202404/11/content_WS6617c858c6d0868f4e8e5f4d.html (China Central Government English site on payment services for overseas visitors)

### 5.6 Visa-Free Transit (240-Hour / 10 Days)

As of December 17, 2024, China upgraded its transit visa exemption to 240 hours (10 days) for nationals of 55 countries (all EU member states, USA, Canada, UK, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, and others). From November 5, 2025, entry via Hong Kong West Kowloon Station (high-speed rail) qualifies for the exemption, making the Guangzhou–Hong Kong HSR corridor a transit-eligible entry point.

**Key rules:**
- You must have confirmed onward international travel booked (flight, train, or ferry) within 240 hours.
- Complete the online Arrival Card via NIA 12367 app or WeChat/Alipay mini-program before boarding.
- You can travel within Guangdong Province and 23 other provinces during the 240-hour window.
- Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months beyond entry date.
- Cannot apply if you have illegal entry/exit records in China within 5 years.

**Source:** https://www.chinadiscovery.com/chinese-visa/240-hour-visa-free-transit.html ; https://www.travelchinaguide.com/tour/visa/free-transit-144-hours.htm

## Part 6: Cross-Border Context — Guangzhou to Hong Kong

### High-Speed Rail: Guangzhou South Station to Hong Kong West Kowloon

The Guangzhou–Hong Kong Express Rail Link is the fastest overland connection between the two cities.

| Route | Journey time | Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Guangzhou South (广州南) to Hong Kong West Kowloon (香港西九龍) | approximately 47 minutes | approximately 35 trains/day |
| Guangzhou East (广州东) to Hong Kong Hung Hom (via conventional intercity) | approximately 2 hours | approximately 4 trains/day |

**Recommended for foreign tourists:** The Guangzhou South to West Kowloon HSR route. Faster, more frequent, and the co-location immigration at West Kowloon Station means you clear both Hong Kong and mainland customs in one building at the Hong Kong end.

### Immigration Process for Foreign Passport Holders

1. Purchase a real-name ticket (passport number required at point of purchase — online at 12306.cn, Trip.com, or station ticket windows).
2. Proceed through mainland immigration clearance at Guangzhou South (departure hall, Floor B1). Allow 45–60 minutes before departure for this process.
3. At Hong Kong West Kowloon: the arrivals hall has both Hong Kong and mainland immigration — complete both in sequence.

**Ticket purchase for foreigners:** Trip.com and Klook both sell tickets with passport input and accept international cards. The Chinese national ticketing app (12306) requires a Chinese phone number for registration, making Trip.com the easiest route for foreign-passport holders.

**240-Hour Visa-Free Transit via HSR:** From November 5, 2025, entering mainland China via West Kowloon on the HSR qualifies for the 240-hour transit exemption for eligible 55-country passport holders with confirmed onward international travel.

**For mixed-passport groups (mainland Chinese + foreign-passport holders):** Mainland Chinese use separate immigration lanes at both ends. Allow extra buffer time if your group has both passport types.

**Source:** https://www.highspeed.mtr.com.hk/en/guide/process-departure.html (Hong Kong MTR High Speed Rail official guide)
