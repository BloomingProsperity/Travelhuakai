## 1. District-by-District Breakdown

### 1.1 Dongcheng District (东城区)

**Why visit**: The single highest-density district for first-time foreign visitors. Contains the Forbidden City, Tiananmen Square, Wangfujing, Nanluoguxiang Hutong, Temple of Heaven (shared with Chaoyang border), Drum Tower, Bell Tower, Lama Temple, and the Confucius Temple — all walkable or one metro stop from each other.

**Cultural character**: The old imperial heart of Beijing. Every alley in this district was designed around the north-south axis of the Yongle Emperor's capital plan. Walking here means walking through 600 years of unbroken urban form.

**Top attractions**:
1. Forbidden City (Palace Museum) — the centrepiece of Beijing
2. Nanluoguxiang — the most accessible hutong for first-time visitors, lined with snack stalls, small cafes, and indie craft shops in a Yuan-dynasty lane grid
3. Lama Temple (Yonghe Gong) — the best-preserved active Tibetan Buddhist temple in China outside Tibet

**Food specialty**: Wangfujing Snack Street and Donghuamen Night Market area; Guijie (Ghost Street, Dongzhimennei) for late-night Sichuan crayfish and hot-pot

**Transport hub**: Lines 1 (Wangfujing, Tiananmen East/West), Line 2 (Jianguomen, Chaoyangmen, Yonghegong), Line 5 (Yonghegong, Tiantandongmen), Line 6 (Nanluoguxiang), Line 8 (Tianqiao)

**Tier**: Must-visit. More historic sites per square kilometre than anywhere else in the city; realistic walking radius covers 5-6 major attractions.

---

### 1.2 Xicheng District (西城区)

**Why visit**: The western counterpart to Dongcheng, less crowded at identical sites. Beihai Park, Jingshan Park, Houhai lakefront bars, Shichahai lakes, Prince Gong's Mansion, and the National Centre for the Performing Arts (NCPA) all sit here. Dashilar and Qianmen commercial street straddle the Xicheng-Dongcheng border.

**Cultural character**: Ming and Qing imperial pleasure grounds — gardens, palace annexes, and noble mansions that served as overflow from the Forbidden City. Houhai is where the capital's nobility once kept their lakeside retreats.

**Top attractions**:
1. Beihai Park — imperial garden surrounding a man-made lake, the White Dagoba visible from Jingshan
2. Houhai lakefront — the hutong bar-and-boat district; best at dusk and after dark
3. National Centre for the Performing Arts (NCPA) — Paul Andreu's titanium-and-glass egg, world-class opera and symphony performances

**Food specialty**: Houhai / Shichahai bar street; Dashilar for old-brand Beijing snacks (Daoxiangcun pastries, Liubiju pickles)

**Transport hub**: Line 4 (Xishimen, Beijing Zoo, Xuanwumen), Line 2 (Fuchengmen, Changchunjie), Line 8 (Nanluoguxiang-Xicheng shared)

**Tier**: Must-visit. Slightly quieter than Dongcheng but equal historical depth; combines well with a same-day Dongcheng itinerary.

---

### 1.3 Chaoyang District (朝阳区)

**Why visit**: Beijing's international district. The Central Business District (CBD), Sanlitun bar and shopping zone, 798 Art District (Dashanzi), embassies, Olympic venues (Bird's Nest, Water Cube), and the Workers' Stadium neighbourhood are all here. The largest district by population in inner Beijing, covering northeast and east of the city.

**Cultural character**: Cosmopolitan and modern. The district that emerged as China opened after 1978 — tower blocks, foreign restaurants, and luxury malls sit alongside former factory art spaces and canal-side parks.

**Top attractions**:
1. 798 Art District (798艺术区) — 50-hectare converted Bauhaus-era military-factory complex, now hosting 200+ galleries, design studios, and concept restaurants
2. Olympic Park — Bird's Nest (National Stadium) and Water Cube (National Aquatics Centre) from the 2008 Games
3. Sanlitun — the most foreigner-friendly street grid for dining, nightlife, and brand shopping

**Food specialty**: Sanlitun Village and Taikoo Li for international dining; Liangmaqiao for upscale expat dining; Chaoyang Park Road for Korean BBQ clusters

**Transport hub**: Lines 10 (Sanlitun, Liangmaqiao, Guomao), Line 6 (Chaoyangmen), Line 1 (Guomao), Line 14 (798 adjacent at Jiangtai/Wangjing), Airport Express Line (Sanyuanqiao)

**Tier**: Worth-it. Essential for non-historic side of Beijing; 798 is a half-day or full-day destination on its own. Olympic Park is compelling but requires dedicated time.

### 1.4 Haidian District (海淀区)

**Why visit**: The Summer Palace and Old Summer Palace (Yuanmingyuan) are the primary draws. Secondary draws: Fragrant Hills (Xiangshan) in autumn, Beijing Botanical Garden, and proximity to Peking and Tsinghua universities.

**Cultural character**: The north-western imperial pleasure grounds — every Qing dynasty emperor from Yongzheng onward maintained a garden complex here, the grandest of which became the Summer Palace after the Old Summer Palace was destroyed in 1860.

**Top attractions**:
1. Summer Palace (颐和园) — UNESCO World Heritage imperial garden, Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill
2. Old Summer Palace / Yuanmingyuan (圆明园) — haunting ruins of the larger Qing complex burnt by Anglo-French forces in 1860; popular with Chinese visitors as a patriotic site
3. Fragrant Hills Park (香山公园) — best visited mid-October when red-leaf maples peak; 557m summit with city views

**Food specialty**: Wudaokou area for Korean and Japanese food (large Korean student population near university zone); Zhongguancun for modern cafes

**Transport hub**: Line 4 (Summer Palace: Xinjiangongmen, North Palace Gate; Peking Univ East Gate; Beijing Botanical Garden); Xijiao Light Rail (for Summer Palace western gate)

**Tier**: Must-visit for Summer Palace. Yuanmingyuan and Fragrant Hills are worth-it additions on a longer itinerary.

---

### 1.5 Fengtai District (丰台区)

**Why visit**: Primarily for the Marco Polo Bridge (Lugouqiao), the oldest surviving bridge in Beijing (built 1189 CE, Jin dynasty), and the adjacent Wanping Fortress — the site of the 1937 incident that triggered the Second Sino-Japanese War. Also houses Beijing West Railway Station, the city's high-speed hub for trains to Xi'an, Wuhan, Guangzhou, and Tibet.

**Cultural character**: Historically the southwestern gateway to the capital. The flat floodplain of the Yongding River gave the district a different character from the imperial hill-and-lake parks of the north and west.

**Top attractions**:
1. Marco Polo Bridge (卢沟桥) — 11-arch granite bridge with over 500 carved stone lions; adjacent Museum of the War of Resistance Against Japanese Aggression
2. Beijing Garden of World Flowers (北京世界花卉大观园) — large themed botanical garden
3. China Aerospace Museum (中国航空博物馆) — for aviation and space enthusiasts

**Food specialty**: No distinct tourist food destination; Fengtai Old Town area has street-food markets

**Transport hub**: Beijing West Railway Station (Line 7, Line 9); Liuliqiao interchange (Lines 9+10)

**Tier**: Worth-it for Marco Polo Bridge only; skip on a short itinerary. Beijing West Station matters for transit. The bridge visit takes 1.5-2 hours and pairs with the war museum.

---

### 1.6 Shijingshan District (石景山区)

**Why visit**: Home to the former Capital Steel (Shougang) industrial park, now repurposed as a major sports and cultural venue after hosting several 2022 Winter Olympics venues. Also the western end of Beijing's urban subway network; forested hills with trails.

**Cultural character**: Heavy-industrial Beijing — the Shougang steel plant operated here from 1919 to 2010; its conversion into an Olympic venue cluster has made it an example of industrial heritage tourism that attracts architecture enthusiasts.

**Top attractions**:
1. Shougang Industrial Park / 2022 Winter Olympics venue cluster — Big Air Shougang ski jump; Wukesong Arena nearby
2. Fahai Temple (法海寺) — rare surviving Ming-dynasty temple with original fresco murals; limited visitor capacity

**Food specialty**: No tourist food district

**Transport hub**: Lines 1 (Gucheng terminus), Line S1 (light rail to Mentougou and Shijingshan)

**Tier**: Skip on first trip unless specifically interested in industrial heritage or Winter Olympics legacy venues. Fahai Temple is a hidden gem for art historians.

---

### 1.7 Mentougou District (门头沟区)

**Why visit**: Mountain scenery, ancient villages, and the western section of the Great Wall. The Jingxi Ancient Road (京西古道), a UNESCO-candidate trade and pilgrimage route, passes through here. Tanzhe Temple (潭柘寺), possibly the oldest Buddhist temple site in the Beijing area, is here.

**Cultural character**: The Western Mountains (西山) are the spiritual backstop to Beijing — coal mines, pilgrim temples, and mountain villages that supplied the imperial capital for centuries.

**Top attractions**:
1. Tanzhe Temple (潭柘寺) — ancient forested temple complex, quiet and atmospheric
2. Jietai Temple (戒台寺) — famous for rare ancient pine trees and dramatic cliff-edge position
3. Jingxi Ancient Road hiking trails

**Food specialty**: Local mountain cuisine in Zhaitang Ancient Village

**Transport hub**: Line S1 light rail (Shijingshan to Mencheng); buses from Pingguoyuan Station (Line 1 terminus). No direct metro to most sites; requires bus or car.

**Tier**: Worth-it for nature and hiking visitors; skip on first short trip. Tanzhe Temple is reachable by public bus (~70 min from Pingguoyuan) and is compelling for those who have exhausted inner-city temples.

---

### 1.8 Fangshan District (房山区)

**Why visit**: Contains two UNESCO World Heritage sites: Peking Man Site at Zhoukoudian (Homo erectus fossils, occupied 500,000-250,000 BP) and part of the Ming Tombs World Heritage grouping. Also home to the Shihua Cave (石花洞).

**Cultural character**: Deep prehistoric and ancient history; the district is the archaeological foundation of human habitation in northern China.

**Top attractions**:
1. Peking Man Site at Zhoukoudian (周口店北京人遗址) — UNESCO World Heritage, museum with fossils and 4D theatre; 42 km southwest
2. Shihua Cave (石花洞) — elaborately decorated limestone cave system; 6 levels open to visitors
3. Cloud Residence Temple (云居寺) — world's largest collection of stone-carved Buddhist sutras (14,278 stone tablets)

**Food specialty**: No tourist food district

**Transport hub**: No direct subway; requires suburban rail to Liangxiang or bus/car from Tianqiao area. Yanfang Line reaches Fangshan town.

**Tier**: Worth-it for Zhoukoudian (UNESCO interest); skip on a standard city itinerary. Getting there takes 1-1.5 hours each way.

---

### 1.9 Tongzhou District (通州区)

**Why visit**: Universal Beijing Resort is the primary foreign-tourist attraction. The Grand Canal Cultural Tourism Area (大运河文化旅游景区, 5A-rated) is a new canal-heritage attraction along the northern end of the Ming-Qing Grand Canal.

**Cultural character**: The historic southern terminus of the Grand Canal, where goods from the Yangtze River delta arrived to supply the imperial capital. Now also the city's planned administrative sub-centre.

**Top attractions**:
1. Universal Beijing Resort (北京环球度假区) — the largest Universal Studios outside the US; six themed zones including Harry Potter and Kung Fu Panda areas
2. Grand Canal Cultural Tourism Area (大运河文化旅游景区) — canal-side heritage zone with Randeng Tower
3. Songzhuang Art District — China's largest artist village, affordable studios and galleries

**Food specialty**: Canal-side restaurants in the Grand Canal zone; Tongzhou Old Town food streets

**Transport hub**: Line 6 (Tongzhou Beiguan, Haomen, Tongzhou Beiyuan); Line 1 extension to Huanqiu Dujiaqu (Universal Resort)

**Tier**: Worth-it specifically for Universal Resort (requires a full day) and Grand Canal (half-day).

---

### 1.10 Shunyi District (顺义区)

**Why visit**: Site of Beijing Capital International Airport (PEK, Terminal 3). No significant cultural tourism targets.

**Cultural character**: Flat agricultural plain northeast of the city; airport and logistics hub.

**Top attractions**: Horse culture / equestrian clubs; Shunyi Olympic Rowing-Canoeing Park

**Food specialty**: None of tourist interest

**Transport hub**: Airport Express Line (Capital Airport T2, T3)

**Tier**: Skip on first trip. Transit district only.

---

### 1.11 Daxing District (大兴区)

**Why visit**: Site of Beijing Daxing International Airport (PKX), designed by Zaha Hadid Architects — the terminal building is architecturally significant. Also home to Beijing Wildlife Park.

**Cultural character**: Southern expansion zone; the airport is architecturally significant (1.7M sq m, radial design, world's largest single-structure airport terminal when opened in 2019).

**Top attractions**:
1. Beijing Daxing International Airport (大兴机场) — terminal building is an architectural spectacle
2. Beijing Wildlife Park (北京野生动物园) — large drive-through safari park

**Food specialty**: None of tourist interest outside airport zone

**Transport hub**: Daxing Airport Express Line (Caoqiao to Daxing Airport, ~20 min)

**Tier**: Skip for tourism; visit only as an airport transit or for architectural interest.

---

### 1.12 Changping District (昌平区)

**Why visit**: Contains the Ming Tombs (十三陵, UNESCO World Heritage) and Juyongguan Great Wall. The Ming Tombs are the burial site of 13 of the 16 Ming emperors.

**Cultural character**: The ceremonial landscape of Ming imperial death — designed to harmonise the tombs with the surrounding Tianshou Mountain ridgeline according to feng shui principles. The 7-km Sacred Way lined with stone animal statues is one of the best examples of Ming spatial planning outside the Forbidden City.

**Top attractions**:
1. Ming Tombs Sacred Way (神道) — 7-km approach avenue with 18 pairs of stone figures
2. Dingling Tomb (定陵) — the only fully excavated Ming emperor's underground palace; CNY 65 peak season
3. Changling Tomb (长陵) — the largest and most visited; houses the original timber Hall of Eminent Favours

**Food specialty**: No dedicated tourist food area

**Transport hub**: No direct subway to Ming Tombs; Tourist Bus 872 from Deshengmen (Xicheng, Line 2 Jishuitan) operates Apr-Oct. Taxi or tour bus most practical.

**Tier**: Worth-it for Ming Tombs (UNESCO); skip Juyongguan Great Wall in favour of Mutianyu or Badaling which are better preserved.

---

### 1.13 Pinggu District (平谷区)

**Why visit**: Easternmost of Beijing's mountain districts. Known for peach orchards (April blossom season), hiking, and the Jingdong Grand Canyon. Niche appeal for outdoor activities.

**Cultural character**: Agricultural mountain district; the "peach capital" of Beijing. Spring blossom (April) and autumn colour (October) are the peak appeal seasons.

**Top attractions**:
1. Jingdong Grand Canyon — narrow gorge with river rafting (summer)
2. Peach blossom viewing in April
3. Great Wall at Gubeikou — wild and unrestored

**Food specialty**: Fresh peaches and mountain produce in season

**Transport hub**: No metro connection; requires long bus or private car from central Beijing (2+ hours).

**Tier**: Skip on first trip. Appeals to Chinese domestic tourists; not a primary foreign tourist draw.

---

### 1.14 Huairou District (怀柔区)

**Why visit**: The access district for Mutianyu Great Wall (the most-recommended section for foreign tourists), the Huanghuacheng Lakeside Great Wall, and Jiankou Wild Wall for experienced hikers. Also contains Hongluo Temple and Yanqi Lake resort area.

**Cultural character**: Mountain and reservoir district that functions as Beijing's "green lung" to the northeast. The Great Wall sections here have better preservation and fewer crowds than Badaling.

**Top attractions**:
1. Mutianyu Great Wall (慕田峪长城) — foreign-tourist favourite; cable car and toboggan available; 1M foreign visitors in 2025
2. Huanghuacheng Lakeside Great Wall (黄花城水长城) — unusual section where the Wall descends into a reservoir
3. Jiankou Wild Wall (箭扣长城) — unrestored; for serious hikers and photographers only

**Food specialty**: Huairou Beidajie town has roadside restaurants; some restaurants at Mutianyu scenic area

**Transport hub**: No direct metro to Mutianyu; Bus 916 from Dongzhimen (Chaoyang) to Huairou Beidajie, then H23 minibus to Mutianyu (total ~2 hours). Private taxi or tour van is the most practical option.

**Tier**: Must-visit for Great Wall. Mutianyu is the recommended first Great Wall experience for foreign tourists.

---

### 1.15 Miyun District (密云区)

**Why visit**: Simatai Great Wall (night tours available) is the premier attraction. Miyun Reservoir is Beijing's main drinking-water reservoir and a large recreational area. The area is also developing boutique homestay tourism.

**Cultural character**: The water tower and mountain boundary of northeastern Beijing. Simatai is the only Great Wall section designated for night tours.

**Top attractions**:
1. Simatai Great Wall (司马台长城) — original Ming-dynasty appearance; night tours April-October; integrated with Gubei Water Town resort
2. Gubei Water Town (古北水镇) — recreated Ming-dynasty water town at the foot of Simatai; hotel resort
3. Miyun Reservoir — boating and waterside hiking trails

**Food specialty**: Gubei Water Town restaurants for a resort-dining experience

**Transport hub**: No direct metro; Suburban bus from Dongzhimen (Bus 980 to Miyun town; then local bus or taxi); ~2 hours. Simatai shuttle buses operate from Dongzhimen and Wangfujing.

**Tier**: Worth-it for Simatai night tour (unique experience); skip Miyun Reservoir on a standard itinerary.

---

### 1.16 Yanqing District (延庆区)

**Why visit**: Badaling Great Wall (most visited section in the world, 65,000 visitor daily cap) is in Yanqing. The 2022 Winter Olympics alpine skiing and sliding venues are open for public skiing. Longqing Gorge (龙庆峡) offers dramatic canyon scenery and annual Ice Lantern Festival (January-February).

**Cultural character**: High plateau (500-600m elevation) northwest of the city, with a noticeably cooler and cleaner-air microclimate than central Beijing. The 2022 Olympics investment has improved infrastructure significantly.

**Top attractions**:
1. Badaling Great Wall (八达岭长城) — the world's most-visited Great Wall section; most accessible but most crowded
2. National Alpine Ski Centre (国家高山滑雪中心) — Olympic-grade slopes open to the public for skiing in winter
3. Longqing Gorge (龙庆峡) — cable car and boat tours through the gorge; ice lantern festival in winter

**Food specialty**: Yanqing town for local specialties; no international restaurant district

**Transport hub**: S2 Suburban Railway from Beijing North Station to Badaling (~1.5 hours); Bus 877/919 from Deshengmen (~1 hour); High-speed rail to Yanqing Station (~30 min from Beijing North, 2022 Olympics line).

**Tier**: Worth-it for Badaling; must-visit for skiers in winter.

## 2. Top 15 Attractions for Foreign Tourists

### Forbidden City / Palace Museum (故宫博物院)

**District**: Dongcheng
**Tier**: must-visit

**Why visit**: The largest intact imperial palace complex in the world — 180 acres, 980 buildings, 8,728 rooms — occupied by 24 emperors across the Ming and Qing dynasties (1420-1912). The collection includes over 1.8 million artefacts. The central axis from Meridian Gate to the Imperial Garden is one of the most dramatic processional spaces on Earth.

**Booking**: Mandatory advance booking at https://bookingticket.dpm.org.cn — no same-day tickets sold at the gate (with the narrow exception of the Donghuamen ticket window for walk-up passport holders, arrive by 7:30 AM on the day). Tickets released 7 days in advance at 20:00 Beijing time. Account created with email + passport number. The 40,000-person daily cap means tickets sell out within minutes on release for peak weekends. Foreign visitors book using passport number; no Chinese phone number strictly required on the official site. Foreign cards (Visa/Mastercard) are not accepted directly on the official site — use Alipay International, WeChat Pay, or book via Trip.com/Klook/Viator with an international card at a small service fee.

**Hours**: High season (Apr 1 - Oct 31): 08:30-17:00, last entry 16:00. Low season (Nov 1 - Mar 31): 08:30-16:30, last entry 15:30. Closed Mondays (except national holidays).

**Daily limit**: 40,000 tickets

**Foreign-card tickets**: partial (via third-party platforms: Trip.com, Klook, Viator)

**English signage**: yes — comprehensive bilingual signage throughout; audio guides available; English-language tours available

**Official URL**: https://intl.dpm.org.cn/visit.html?l=en

### Tiananmen Square (天安门广场)

**District**: Dongcheng (border with Xicheng)
**Tier**: must-visit

**Why visit**: The world's third-largest public square (440,000 sq m) and the symbolic centre of the People's Republic. Flanked by the Gate of Heavenly Peace (with Mao's portrait), the National Museum of China, and the Great Hall of the People. The daily flag-raising ceremony at sunrise and flag-lowering at sunset draw crowds year-round.

**Booking**: Free entry but mandatory advance reservation (1-7 days ahead) via WeChat mini-program or the Beijing government official booking page (requires valid ID/passport registration). Walk-up visitors without a reservation will be turned back at security checkpoints.

**Hours**: 05:00-22:00 (approximate; closes roughly 1 hour after the evening flag-lowering ceremony). Flag-raising is at exact sunrise time; flag-lowering at exact sunset.

**Daily limit**: No published cap; reservation is mandatory but quota appears large

**Foreign-card tickets**: N/A (free)

**English signage**: limited — perimeter signs are bilingual; on-site staff rarely speak English

**Official URL**: https://english.beijing.gov.cn/travellinginbeijing/
**Image (public-domain/CC)**: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/Tiananmen_Square.JPG

### Great Wall at Mutianyu (慕田峪长城)

**District**: Huairou
**Tier**: must-visit (top recommendation for foreign tourists)

**Why visit**: The section most recommended by experienced foreign travellers. Well-restored Ming masonry, 23 watchtowers across a 2.25 km open stretch, two cable-car options (standard gondola or open chair lift), and an optional toboggan descent. Far less crowded than Badaling on weekdays. In 2025, Mutianyu recorded 1 million foreign inbound visitors — a 60% year-on-year increase, ranking 2nd among Asia's top attractions on Tripadvisor. The official English website accepts international cards directly.

**Booking**: No advance reservation required — walk-up tickets available. Online booking at https://en.mutianyugreatwall.com accepts international cards. Each ID limited to one ticket per day.

**Hours**: Peak season (Mar 16 - Nov 15): 07:30-18:00. Off-peak season (Nov 16 - Mar 15): 08:00-17:30. Night tours (Apr 30 - Oct): 17:00-21:00, last entry 20:30.

**Prices**: Entrance CNY 45 adult; cable car CNY 100 round trip; toboggan CNY 100 one-way; chair lift CNY 60 one-way.

**Daily limit**: No published cap; arrive before 10:00 on peak weekends to avoid queues

**Foreign-card tickets**: yes (official English website accepts international cards directly)

**English signage**: yes — signage and staff communication in English throughout; English audio guides available

**Official URL**: https://en.mutianyugreatwall.com
**Image (public-domain/CC)**: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fa/Great_Wall_of_China_July_2006.JPG

### Great Wall at Badaling (八达岭长城)

**District**: Yanqing
**Tier**: worth-it (most accessible section; recommended when Mutianyu is unavailable)

**Why visit**: The world's most-visited Great Wall section and the first opened to tourists (1957). Closest wall section to central Beijing (70 km), with express bus and the scenic S2 heritage railway from Beijing North. The wall here is heavily restored and wide enough to handle the crowds. Most visiting heads of state are taken to Badaling.

**Booking**: Advance booking required. Tickets released 15 days in advance. Passport required for booking; foreign visitors must collect a printed ticket at the service window at the entrance before entering. Book at https://english.beijing.gov.cn/specials/ticketing/attractions/202407/t20240717_3751609.html (official) or via Trip.com/Klook for English-language international-card booking.

**Hours**: Peak season (Apr 1 - Oct 31): 06:30-18:30. Off season (Nov 1 - Mar 31): 07:30-17:00.

**Daily limit**: 65,000 visitors

**Foreign-card tickets**: partial (via third-party platforms)

**English signage**: yes — bilingual throughout; English audio guides available for rent

**Official URL**: https://english.beijing.gov.cn/specials/ticketing/attractions/202407/t20240717_3751609.html

### Great Wall at Simatai (司马台长城)

**District**: Miyun
**Tier**: worth-it (for travellers on 4+ day itineraries seeking a night tour)

**Why visit**: The only section of the Great Wall with authorised night tours, running April-October. Original Ming Dynasty masonry with minimal restoration; UNESCO has cited it as the "Original Great Wall." Integrated with the Gubei Water Town resort (upscale hotel complex at the base). The night-lit wall reflected in the river below is photographically spectacular.

**Booking**: Advance booking strongly recommended, especially for evening tours. Book via https://www.simatai.com (Chinese) or through GetYourGuide/Viator for international booking. Located 125 km northeast of Beijing (~2.5 hours by car).

**Hours**: Day: 09:00-17:00. Night tours (Apr-Oct, weekdays): 17:30-21:10; (weekends): 17:30-21:40.

**Prices**: Day ticket CNY 40-80; Night tour CNY 80-120.

**Daily limit**: No published cap; night tours sell out — book 1-2 weeks ahead

**Foreign-card tickets**: via third-party platforms (GetYourGuide, Viator)

**English signage**: limited — Gubei Water Town has English menus; Wall signage is primarily Chinese

**Official URL**: https://www.simatai.com

### Summer Palace (颐和园)

**District**: Haidian
**Tier**: must-visit

**Why visit**: The best-preserved imperial garden in China and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built around the 220-hectare Kunming Lake with Longevity Hill as a backdrop, the Summer Palace is a 2.9 sq km theatrical landscape designed to evoke natural scenery while accommodating imperial court life. The Long Corridor (728m painted gallery) is one of the most photographed architectural features in China. Audio guides available in 19 languages.

**Booking**: Via "Summer Palace" WeChat Official Account with passport or permanent residence permit; also via Visiting Beijing Parks website https://www.bjgyol.com.cn (English available). No mandatory advance booking for most visits, but WeChat booking allows up to 7 days' advance purchase.

**Hours**: Peak season (Apr 1 - Oct 31): Gate 06:00-20:00 (last entry 19:00); interior attractions 08:00-18:00 (last entry 17:30). Off season (Nov 1 - Mar 31): Gate 06:30-19:00 (last entry 18:00); interior attractions 08:30-17:00 (last entry 16:30). Closed Mondays (except statutory holidays).

**Prices**: Admission CNY 30 peak / CNY 20 off-season. Combined ticket (includes Tower of Fragrance, Garden of Virtue and Harmony, Suzhou Street): CNY 60 peak / CNY 50 off-season.

**Daily limit**: No published cap

**Foreign-card tickets**: partial (foreign-card acceptance on the Visiting Beijing Parks platform has had inconsistencies — confirm before travel; Trip.com accepts foreign cards reliably)

**English signage**: yes — bilingual throughout; audio guides in 19 languages available; English-speaking guides available at three gate locations

**Official URL**: https://english.beijing.gov.cn/specials/parktours/guidevisitors/summerpalace/

### Temple of Heaven (天坛)

**District**: Dongcheng
**Tier**: must-visit

**Why visit**: The largest extant sacrificial altar complex in China, built 1406-1420 and used by every Ming and Qing emperor to pray for good harvests. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (Qinian Dian) — a triple-eaved circular blue-roofed timber structure — is one of the most architecturally perfect buildings in Chinese history. The park grounds double as a morning exercise venue; at dawn locals practice tai chi, erhu, and ballroom dancing under the cypress trees.

**Booking**: Via WeChat Official Account "Temple of Heaven Official Ticketing Platform" with passport. Tickets open 7 days in advance. Walk-up tickets available at the gate for the park grounds; the Hall of Prayer requires a combined ticket.

### Lama Temple / Yonghe Gong (雍和宫)

## 3. Food Districts

### 3.1 Wangfujing Snack Street and Donghuamen Area (王府井小吃街 / 东华门)

**Location**: Dongcheng, east of Forbidden City
**What food culture**: The most internationally famous Beijing street-food destination. Following recent renovations, the original Wangfujing Snack Alley has scaled down its "exotic foods on a stick" stalls and repositioned toward cultural souvenirs, but Donghuamen Night Market nearby and Xianyukou Food Street (咸鱼口美食街) still serve traditional Beijing snacks: jianbing (savoury crepe), tanghulu (candied hawthorn), scallion pancakes, lamb skewers, and Beijing sweet dough sticks (youtiao). The adjacent Wangfujing Department Store food hall is comprehensive and air-conditioned. Note that the "scorpions and insects on sticks" displays are primarily tourist theatre — not representative of what locals eat.

**English menu**: Partial — tourist-facing stalls have photo menus; dedicated English menus rare
**Walk-in feasibility**: Very easy; no reservations needed
**Peak hours**: 17:00-21:00 daily; weekends and holidays are crush-crowded

---

### 3.2 Guijie — Ghost Street (鬼街, 东直门内大街)

**Location**: Dongcheng, Dongzhimennei Dajie
**What food culture**: The city's primary late-night dining street — 1.4 km of restaurants open until 02:00 or 04:00, decorated with red lanterns year-round. Over 150 restaurants, 90% of which are catering establishments. Signature dish: spicy Sichuan-style crayfish (麻辣小龙虾) by weight, also mala hot pot, Wanzhou roast fish, and bullfrogs. Located in the embassy belt, the street has a long tradition of welcoming foreign diners. Best visited on a weeknight when locals are the primary clientele rather than other tourists.

**English menu**: yes in major restaurants; staff often have basic English or photo menus
**Walk-in feasibility**: Easy; most restaurants accept walk-ins; peak times may require a short wait
**Peak hours**: 19:00-23:00; many restaurants open until 02:00+

---

### 3.3 Sanlitun (三里屯)

**Location**: Chaoyang, northeast embassy district
**What food culture**: Beijing's most international dining and nightlife zone. The Taikoo Li shopping complex and adjacent streets contain every major international cuisine alongside high-end Chinese restaurants. Brunch culture is strong on weekends. The area caters overwhelmingly to expats and foreign visitors — English menus universal. Bar Street (酒吧街) along north Sanlitun is the traditional nightlife focus.

**English menu**: yes — universal
**Walk-in feasibility**: Easy; popular restaurants may have waits on weekend evenings
**Peak hours**: Weekend brunch 10:00-13:00; dinner 19:00-22:00; bar scene 21:00 onward

---

### 3.4 Houhai / Shichahai Hutong Area (后海/什刹海)

**Location**: Xicheng, northwest of Beihai Park
**What food culture**: Old Beijing hutong-bar culture. Restaurants and bars line the north and south shores of the three linked Shichahai lakes. Traditional food includes: Beijing duck (several historic restaurants here), old-style sour plum drinks (酸梅汤), mung-bean jelly (豌豆黄), and zhajiangmian (fermented soybean-paste noodles). Bar-terrace dining with lake views is the primary foreign-tourist experience. Yandaixiejie (Tobacco Pipe Alley) is lined with small cafes and craft shops.

**English menu**: Partial — tourist-facing lakeside bars and restaurants have English menus; side-lane local restaurants do not
**Walk-in feasibility**: Easy; very busy after 17:00
**Peak hours**: Sunset onward (18:00-22:00 in summer); morning tai chi locals at 06:00-08:00

---

### 3.5 Nanluoguxiang (南锣鼓巷)

**Location**: Dongcheng, north of the Drum Tower
**What food culture**: The most walkable hutong food street for first-time visitors — 800m north-south lane with snack stalls and small restaurants in Ming and Qing courtyard buildings. Signature local snacks: tanghulu, fried squid, jianbing, and donkey-meat burger (驴肉火烧). Modern dessert shops (cheese teas, Taiwanese shaved ice) coexist with century-old recipes. Evening lantern-lit atmosphere.

**English menu**: Partial — photo menus universal; dedicated English text menus less common
**Walk-in feasibility**: Easy; no reservations needed for most
**Peak hours**: 14:00-20:00; crush-crowded on weekends and Golden Week

---

### 3.6 Liangmaqiao (亮马桥)

**Location**: Chaoyang, near the embassy district
**What food culture**: Beijing's upscale international dining corridor, popular with the diplomatic community and high-income expats. Korean BBQ, Japanese omakase, French brasseries, and rooftop cocktail bars are concentrated here. Liangmaqiao Road and the surrounding Yansha mall area offer a comprehensive range of non-Chinese cuisines at above-average price points.

**English menu**: yes — universal
**Walk-in feasibility**: Easier on weekdays; reservations recommended for popular restaurants on weekend evenings
**Peak hours**: Dinner 19:00-22:00; brunch 10:00-13:00 weekends

## 5. Practical Pitfalls Specific to Beijing

### Pitfall 1: Forbidden City — The Ticket Scarcity Problem
The daily cap of 40,000 visitors combined with tickets released only 7 days in advance at 20:00 Beijing time means that peak weekends (April-October, all holidays, and Golden Week) can be fully booked within minutes of release. Foreign visitors face an additional barrier: the official booking system at https://bookingticket.dpm.org.cn requires a passport number and the payment step typically rejects foreign Visa/Mastercard issued outside China.

Practical workarounds in order of reliability:
1. Book via Trip.com or Klook 7 days out (they hold allocations and accept international cards)
2. Check again at 22:00 on the 7-day-out day — uncompleted no-show orders often free up slots
3. On the day, walk to the Donghuamen Gate (east side) ticket window by 07:30 with passport — a small on-day passport quota is informally available but is not officially advertised and cannot be guaranteed
4. Never buy from scalpers near the gate — counterfeit tickets are common and you will be denied entry

### Pitfall 2: Summer Palace — The Closing Time Trap
The Summer Palace is a 2.9 sq km complex. Many visitors arrive at 14:00 and underestimate the size. The interior attractions (Tower of Fragrance of Buddha, Garden of Virtue and Harmony, Suzhou Street) stop admitting visitors at 17:30 in peak season — meaning if you arrive at 14:30 and walk to the far end of Kunming Lake, the hill pavilions will be closed before you reach them. Allow a minimum of 4 hours; a full day is not excessive. The park entrance gates close at 20:00 peak season, so an arrival before noon gives a complete visit.

### Pitfall 3: Great Wall — Unauthorised Sections and Scams
Near Badaling and other sections, drivers in car parks and outside bus stops offer "shortcuts" to climb unrestored, unauthorised sections of the Wall. These are illegal under the Beijing Great Wall Protection and Management Regulations; violators face fines, and the unrestored sections have genuinely dangerous unguarded drops and crumbling masonry. At Badaling, touts also offer to sell tickets outside the gate at "discount" — these are either counterfeit or void. Always buy at the official ticket window or pre-book online.

A second scam operates at Beijing railway stations: drivers approach foreign tourists offering a "full day tour" to the Great Wall that turns out to be Juyongguan or an unofficial section, followed by compulsory stops at jade or silk factories with kickback arrangements. Use public Bus 877/916 or a licensed tour operator booked in advance to avoid this.

### Pitfall 4: Universal Beijing Resort — Separate Ticketing System
Universal Beijing Resort operates its own ticketing platform entirely separate from the rest of Beijing's attraction ecosystem. Foreign tourists cannot book Universal through the standard Beijing municipal portals. The official English booking site is https://www.universalbeijingresort.com/en. The park uses real-name ticketing — each ticket is linked to one passport number and can only be used once. Reseller tickets on third-party apps may be non-transferable and can result in denied entry. On peak weekends and Chinese school holidays the park reaches virtual capacity with 2-3 hour waits for popular rides; visit Tuesday-Thursday during non-holiday periods for the best experience.

### Pitfall 5: Tiananmen Square — Reservation Required (Widely Overlooked by Foreign Visitors)
Since 2019, all visitors to Tiananmen Square (not just the Forbidden City — the open-air square itself) must make a free reservation 1-7 days in advance. Foreign tourists routinely arrive at the square perimeter without a reservation and are turned back by security checkpoints with airport-level screening. The reservation system requires a Chinese mobile number for the primary WeChat mini-program interface, which creates a barrier for visitors without local SIMs. Workarounds: ask hotel staff to make the reservation on your behalf using the hotel's WeChat account, or obtain a local SIM card before travelling (now possible via online pre-application per the 2025 tourism measures).

### Pitfall 6: Payment — Preparing Alipay Before Arrival
As of mid-2024, 335+ Beijing metro stations accept foreign contactless bank cards (Mastercard, Visa). However, many smaller restaurants, street stalls, temple gate counters, and suburban market vendors still only accept WeChat Pay or Alipay. Visitors who arrive unprepared for mobile payment encounter repeated friction.

Recommended preparation before departure:
1. Install Alipay International and link a foreign-issued Visa or Mastercard (no Chinese bank account required since 2023)
2. Set up the Alipay "Transport" QR code for Beijing metro in the app (one-time city-specific setup)
3. Obtain the BEIJING PASS smart card at the airport on arrival — covers subway, buses, airport express, taxis, and entry at 30 attractions; purchase with passport only
4. Carry CNY 300-500 in small notes (¥10-50 bills) for markets, temples, and rural-area purchases

Official source for metro foreign-card acceptance: https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202409/18/content_WS66ea148fc6d0868f4e8eb0ae.html

### Pitfall 7: Air Quality — When to Reschedule Outdoor Plans
Beijing's air quality has improved markedly since the 2013-2017 period but PM2.5 levels still regularly exceed WHO guidelines, particularly November-February (coal-heating season in surrounding Hebei) and during spring dust storms (March-April) from Inner Mongolia.

Practical protocol:
- Download IQAir (https://www.iqair.com/china/beijing) or AQICN (https://aqicn.org) for real-time and 3-day forecasts
- AQI (PM2.5) 0-100: all outdoor activities proceed normally
- AQI 100-150: consider N95 mask for prolonged outdoor Wall or park hikes
- AQI above 150: reschedule open-air hikes and palace walks to indoor alternatives (National Museum, 798 galleries, NCPA, malls)
- AQI above 200: indoor-only day recommended
- October is statistically the best month balancing air quality with favourable temperatures; July-August has the most reliably clean air but is hot
