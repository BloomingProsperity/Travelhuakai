## Section A — 小红书 (Xiaohongshu / RedNote, xiaohongshu.com)

### A1. Platform overview and tourism content shape

Xiaohongshu (marketed internationally as RedNote) is China's dominant lifestyle discovery platform, combining Instagram-style image-and-text notes (笔记) with a TikTok-style algorithmic feed. As of 2025 it reports 300+ million monthly active users. Travel content grew 273% from 2023 to 2024, and in May 2024 alone there were nearly 500 million travel-related searches. Over 80% of Chinese travelers now consult Xiaohongshu before trips.

What travel content looks like:
- A note is a post mixing 1-9 images or a short video with structured text: itinerary, transport tips, cost breakdown, restaurant names, booking advice, 避坑 (pitfall warnings).
- Notes are tagged with city names (#北京 #上海 #广州 #深圳) plus activity tags.
- The platform functions as a search engine first: users type 北京 citywalk路线 or 上海周末去哪 and get curated notes ranked by algorithm.
- Collections (收藏) indicate high utility — a single citywalk post for Shanghai 武康路-安福路 route collected 20,000+ saves.

Dominant travel hashtags for 北上广深:
- #北京citywalk / #北京周末 / #北京胡同 / #北京好去处
- #上海citywalk / #上海周末去哪儿 / #上海宝藏地点
- #广州citywalk / #广州永庆坊 / #广州美食
- #深圳citywalk / #深圳周末 / #深圳文和友

Three dominant meta-categories (2024-2025 platform data):
- #CityWalk (urban slow-walking exploration)
- #文化旅游 (cultural/heritage travel)
- #特种兵旅游 (high-density military-style tourism — maximum sites in minimum time)

### A2. Popular tourism creators (2025-2026)

Chinese-language travel KOLs active on Xiaohongshu:
- 莫莉 (Molly) — fashion-travel crossover, large female following
- 纪卜心 (Ji Bu Xin) — outdoor gear + adventure travel, targets active travel demographic
- City-based local recommendation accounts (本地达人) for each of 北上广深 — highest-utility content because they post hyper-local spots

### A3. The TikTok refugee wave (January 2025)

What happened: When the US TikTok ban loomed in January 2025, American users migrated en masse to Xiaohongshu. US app downloads rose more than 20x in the week starting January 8, 2025. More than 700,000 new accounts were created in two days. Xiaohongshu briefly became #1 in the US App Store.

What it produced:
- Short-lived cross-cultural exchange: Chinese users asked Americans for cat photos; Americans compared grocery prices and healthcare costs.
- Xiaohongshu added in-app English translation and bilingual subtitles.
- The wave did NOT produce a sustained English-speaking traveller community. Censorship friction (LGBT content removed, posts by accounts of color suppressed) and the TikTok ban not being enforced caused most refugees to return or go dormant.

For our site: Confirmed global discoverability and international phone number support. English-language travel content for 北上广深 on the platform remains sparse in practice.

### A4. Public web access (xiaohongshu.com vs app)

Web (xiaohongshu.com):
- Publicly accessible globally without a VPN.
- Non-logged-in visitors can read individual notes when navigated to via direct URL.
- Browse discovery (scrolling feed, search) requires login.
- Signup and profile management must be done through the mobile app.

App (guest/visitor mode):
- The app offers visitor mode (游客模式) from the login screen, allowing browsing and searching without an account but without interaction (no saves, no comments).

Bottom line for our site: We can link directly to specific Xiaohongshu note URLs (permanent and publicly readable when accessed directly). We cannot embed a live feed or search widget without authenticated API access, which is not publicly available.

### A5. Foreigner-friendly changes (2024-2025)

Changes confirmed as of 2025:
- No Chinese phone number required: International phone numbers from most countries accepted.
- Social login options: Apple ID (iOS), Google account, WeChat, Weibo, QQ.
- Interface language: English, Japanese, Korean, Spanish, French, Russian UI available.

### A6. Legal — RedNote ToS on third-party reposting

Key rules from Xiaohongshu User Terms of Service (effective January 19, 2025):

Prohibited on-platform:
- Sharing content that infringes on copyright without authorization (photos, videos, text).
- Screenshotting or downloading others content for secondary publishing (pseudo-original porting).
- Posting links to external platforms, QR codes, or contact information that redirect traffic away from Xiaohongshu. Users who redirect to third-party platforms risk account muting.

Not addressed in ToS (neither permitted nor prohibited):
- Whether external websites can hyperlink to Xiaohongshu posts.
- Paraphrasing or summarizing note content on an external site.

Practical guidance for our site:
- Linking out to a specific Xiaohongshu note URL: Permitted (standard hyperlinking, not prohibited by ToS).
- Reproducing text or images from a note on our site: Requires licensing agreement from the author.
- Embedding note content or running an automated scraper: Prohibited.
- Paraphrasing tips with attribution: Legally grey — underlying facts are not copyrightable, but creative expression of the note author is.

### A7. Specific trends — 周末去哪儿, CITYWALK, 露营

周末去哪儿 (Where to go this weekend):
- A common search query typed by users rather than a formal platform feature. Xiaohongshu does not have a branded weekly trending list the way Weibo does.
- The closest equivalent is the 热门话题 (hot topics) section, which includes city-tagged weekend content.
- In May 2024 alone, the platform recorded nearly 500 million travel searches.

CITYWALK (城市漫步):
- Xiaohongshu is the originating platform for the Chinese CITYWALK trend. The English romanization is used on the platform with no Chinese alternative displacing it.
- Trajectory: content began in 2022, viral breakout mid-2023. By June 2023 there were 27 million citywalk-tagged posts; by late July 2023 the count reached 47 million (74% growth in six weeks).
- July 12, 2023: Baidu Index began tracking citywalk as a standalone term — mainstream breakthrough signal.
- By 2024, Xiaohongshu citywalk content had nearly doubled again.
- Viral content model: shifted from route descriptions to emotionally resonant narratives linking citywalk to MBTI personality types, solitude, and slow living.

City-specific canonical routes documented on the platform:
- Shanghai: 武康路-安福路 (Wukang Road-Anfu Road) — most shared citywalk route in China
- Beijing: 东四街-北新桥-五道营-国子监 (East 4th-Beixinqiao-Wudaoying-Guozijian), hutong focus
- Guangzhou: 永庆坊-沙面-广汇楼-沿江西路 (Yongqing Lane-Shamian-Guanghui Building-Yanjiang West Road)
- Shenzhen: Less documented; OCT Harbour (欢乐海岸) and Shenzhen Bay corridor appear frequently

露营 (Camping) trend:
- Emerged during COVID-era restrictions (2021-2022) as substitution for blocked international travel.
- Became a glamping aesthetic trend: high-production photography of curated camp setups, equipment reviews, countryside cafe day trips.
- Drove suburban day-trip content: Huairou/Miyun for Beijing, Zhejiang countryside for Shanghai, Conghua/Zhaoqing for Guangzhou, Dapeng for Shenzhen.
- Post-2023: peaked and split into glamping (高端露营), hiking (徒步), and frisbee (飞盘).
- Academic coverage: aesthetic camping as urban lifestyle phenomenon in Continuum journal (2025).

Sources: https://www.digitaling.com/articles/957039.html | https://socialbeta.com/article/108945 | https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10304312.2025.2499968

## Section B — 马蜂窝 (Mafengwo, mafengwo.cn)

### B1. Platform history — 2010s peak vs current position

Mafengwo was founded in 2006 by Chen Gang and Lv Gang as a pure travel UGC community. Evolution:
- 2006-2010: Community forum for independent travelers (自由行), known for detailed trip reports (游记).
- 2010: Rebranded as travel e-commerce platform while keeping community features.
- 2016: Established as a full OTA with 100+ million registered users.
- 2017: Peak community health; 140 million registered users, dominant in long-form travel content.
- 2020-present: Lost ground to Xiaohongshu (faster growth among younger users) and Douyin (captured short-video travel discovery). Core demographic aged up; younger travelers defaulted to Xiaohongshu.

Current position (2024-2025):
- Xiaohongshu is consulted by 52% of Chinese travelers for destination information; Mafengwo by 22%. (TTG Asia survey, May 2024.)
- Mafengwo retains a differentiated position in long-form, depth-first travel content that Xiaohongshu has not displaced.
- Platform reported 700,000+ long-form content pieces created monthly and 10 million short-form pieces, with 3TB+ daily data — active (not stale) UGC.
- AI features added: AI Roadbook (AI路书) launched to all users mid-2024; DeepSeek integration announced February 12, 2025 (first in the travel industry to integrate DeepSeek).

Sources: https://www.ttgasia.com/2024/05/14/xiaohongshu-is-most-consulted-by-chinese-travellers-whose-travel-sentiments-are-improving/ | https://dragontrail.com/resources/blog/mafengwo-2024-spring-festival-tourism-data-report | https://medium.com/@tony_57148/what-is-mafengwo-where-long-form-content-meets-chinese-ota-709bf1ac89fa

### B2. Content architecture — 攻略 depth

Mafengwo content categories:

| Category | Chinese | Description | Depth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Travel guide | 攻略 | Professional + UGC destination overviews | High — structured by attraction, transport, food, costs |
| Trip diary | 游记 | Long-form personal narratives with photos | Very high — often thousands of words |
| Q&A | 问答 | Community questions and answers | Medium |
| Routes | 旅游线路 | Packaged itineraries (commercial + UGC) | Medium |
| Short notes | 短游记 / 嗡嗡 | Photo + short text (Instagram-like) | Low |
| Videos | 视频 | Short travel videos | Low-medium |

Is the 攻略 content maintained or stale?
- Beijing was the top domestic city on Mafengwo during the 2024 Spring Festival.
- The platform AI local guide covers Beijing among 16 supported destinations with real-time recommendations.
- Content for Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen is actively maintained.
- The depth-first 游记 format (personal trip diaries) tends to lag 6-12 months behind current conditions for fast-changing neighborhoods.

Most useful category for foreigners: 攻略 (structured destination guides). They cover transport from airports, SIM cards, payment systems, neighborhoods, and attractions — precisely the practical information a foreign-tourist site needs. 游记 are useful for inspiration. 问答 surfaces practical edge cases (e.g., Can foreigners enter XX attraction without a Chinese ID?).

### B3. International / English version

Mafengwo does NOT have a public English-language interface. The entire platform (web and app) operates in Simplified Chinese only. There is no international version app.

The platform appears in international travel industry contexts (member of World Tourism Alliance), but its content and UI remain Chinese-language-only. The AI translation features launched mid-2024 (Menu Translation, Multilingual Real-time Translation) are for travelers using the app in-destination, not for the platform UI itself.

### B4. Public web access and login requirements

Based on platform behavior and multiple source descriptions:
- 攻略 pages: Publicly readable without login on mafengwo.cn. Structured destination pages indexed by Baidu and accessible via direct URL.
- 游记 (trip diaries): Publicly readable without login in most cases.
- 问答: Publicly readable. Participation (asking, answering) requires login.
- Booking and commercial functions: Login required.
- The app offers more frictionless discovery; the website works without an account for read purposes.

For our site: Mafengwo 攻略 URLs are stable, publicly accessible, and linkable. Linking to a Beijing 攻略 page is the safest and most useful referral approach.

## Section C — 知乎 (Zhihu, zhihu.com)

### C1. Travel topic structure

Zhihu organizes content around topics (话题) and questions. Travel-relevant topics for 北上广深:
- 北京旅游 — follows: 100,000s; questions range from 首次来北京必去哪里 to 北京哪些景点不值得去
- 上海旅游 — similarly large; heavy foreigner participation visible in question phrasing
- 广州旅游 — smaller but active; food-travel questions dominate
- 深圳旅游 — smallest of the four; focuses on weekend escapes and cross-border day trips to Hong Kong

Additionally relevant:
- 在中国生活的外国人 (Foreigners living in China) — direct source for expat practical advice
- 中国城市 (Chinese cities) — broad topic with cross-city comparisons useful for foreign tourists

Zhihu travel question format captures the things no guidebook tells you: questions like 上海地铁高峰期有多挤 (How crowded is Shanghai metro during rush hour) get detailed, experience-based answers from locals and expats.

### C2. Q&A pattern — foreigners asking 北上广深 questions

This is Zhihu's highest-value content type for our site. Typical patterns:
- 第一次去上海必打卡的地方有哪些? — gets 30-80 answers, many from longtime Shanghai residents and expats.

- 深圳和广州哪个更适合外国人生活? — comparative questions useful for framing destination content.
- Answers are upvoted and ranked by community; the top 3-5 answers on major questions are reliably high-quality synthesized local knowledge.

### C5. Top Zhihu contributors (大V) for 北上广深 travel

Specific named 大V for travel content are not publicly tracked in English-language sources. From platform structure:
- Top contributors are identified by follower counts on profile pages (accessible from any well-answered travel question).
- Accounts with 万+ (10,000+) followers answering Beijing/Shanghai travel questions are the target segment.
- Recommended discovery method: Search 北京旅游 or 上海旅游 on Zhihu, sort by 热门 (popular), and identify recurring answerers with highest vote counts — these are the de facto 大V for that topic.

### C6. Zhihu ToS on third-party reposting

Zhihu Platform User Service Agreement (effective September 15, 2023, revised edition):
- Zhihu has a comprehensive IP protection system: unauthorized scraping, data mining, and unapproved commercial use are explicitly prohibited.
- The agreement prohibits unauthorized technical means to extract content.
- User-generated content (answers, articles) — copyright remains with the individual author; Zhihu holds a license to display it on the platform.

For our site:
- Linking to a Zhihu Q&A thread: Permitted (standard hyperlinking; not addressed in ToS as prohibited).
- Quoting a brief excerpt with attribution: Falls under fair use principles under Chinese copyright law (合理使用), acceptable for commentary/educational purposes, but the quoted portion must be minimal.
- Reproducing full answers: Requires permission from the individual author (not Zhihu itself).
- Paraphrasing factual information from an answer: The underlying facts are not copyrightable; the specific expression is.

## Section E — Trend-Decoding Skills: 小红书 + 抖音 + B站

### E1. How experienced 在华老外 find trending content

Primary monitoring stack used by foreigners living in China:

1. Xiaohongshu app in visitor/guest mode — search city name + keyword (e.g., 上海 周末 2025) without account; read notes sorted by newest. Surfaces hyper-local content 1-4 weeks before it reaches English-language aggregators.
2. Douyin same-city feed (同城) — set location to the target city; the algorithm surfaces local business promotions, newly viral spots, and food trends within hours of posting.
3. WeChat Moments + WeChat Official Accounts — closed ecosystem but highest-trust peer recommendations; requires social network access.
4. Bilibili (B站) — for longer-form vlog content and destination deep-dives; travel vlogs here tend to be 10-20 minutes covering neighborhoods rather than single spots.

For 北上广深 trend intelligence, food influencers (美食博主) tend to lead venue trends — a restaurant going viral on Xiaohongshu or Douyin is the leading indicator for a neighborhood becoming a citywalk destination. City-specific 本地生活 (local life) accounts on Xiaohongshu are the most actionable starting point: search #上海本地人推荐 to surface these.

Sources: https://www.travelofchina.com/what-is-douyin/ | https://chozan.co/chinese-social-media-platforms/ | https://influchina.com/chinese-social-media/

### E2. The platform-to-platform trend migration pattern

Documented pattern using CITYWALK as case study:

1. Xiaohongshu originates the aesthetic/lifestyle framing (image-heavy posts, narrative captions). Hashtag grows organically for 2-6 weeks.
2. Douyin picks up the trend in short-video format — creators film themselves doing the activity, reaching algorithmically distributed audiences 10x larger.
3. Baidu Index begins tracking the keyword (indicates search-engine-scale awareness) — mainstream breakthrough signal. Typically 4-8 weeks after Xiaohongshu origination.
4. Mainstream media (China Daily, People Daily lifestyle sections) write explainers.
5. Bilibili (B站) produces retrospective deep-dive essays and vlogs.
6. The hashtag on Xiaohongshu stabilizes (volume plateaus) 6-10 weeks after Douyin picks it up.

For our site: The most actionable implication is that Douyin virality followed by Xiaohongshu hashtag stabilization is the window where a foreign-tourist site can reference trending content with confidence it is real and sustained, not a 48-hour spike.

Source: Synthesized from citywalk trajectory data in https://www.digitaling.com/articles/957039.html and Baidu Index tracking documentation in https://socialbeta.com/article/108945

### E3. Specific 2025-2026 trends that have stabilized

CITYWALK (城市漫步):
- Status: Fully mainstream as of 2024. No longer a trend — it is a permanent content category.
- City-specific routes are now a standard Xiaohongshu content type.
- Shanghai leads volume; Beijing second (hutong focus); Guangzhou third (colonial-era streets).
- For our site: This vocabulary and route format is the most accessible entry point — we can reference specific citywalk routes knowing users understand the concept.

露营 (Camping) to 飞盘 (Frisbee) to Pickleball:
- Camping peaked 2021-2022, now a mature (slower-growing) activity. The glamping aesthetic persists on Xiaohongshu.
- Flying disc (飞盘) exploded 2022-2023: 80,000+ posts on Xiaohongshu; frisbee sales grew 700% YoY 2022-2023. Associated with the 飞盘媛 (frisbee socialite) aesthetic — women in yoga outfits photographed mid-throw.
- By 2024-2025, frisbee participation normalized; pickleball emerged as the next racket sport trend.
- For our site: Frisbee parks and pickleball courts in 北上广深 are now known local meeting points — relevant for a how-to-meet-locals section.

Sources: https://www.ceoinsightsasia.com/business-inside/into-the-scenes-of-china-s-craze-for-frisbees-nwid-10415.html | https://www.semafor.com/article/03/02/2023/frisbee-sales-boom-in-china

老钱风 (Old Money Aesthetic) cafes and venues:
- Emerged 2023-2024 on Xiaohongshu as a fashion trend; by 2024-2025 it migrated into venue design — cafes, bookstores, and bars adopting muted palettes, vintage furniture, and understated luxury aesthetics.
- Related term: 懒富风 (lazy rich aesthetic).
- 老钱风 is now a search category on Xiaohongshu that surfaces cafe and bar recommendations. Searching #老钱风咖啡 returns venue content for each city.
- Not a single destination — it is a visual category spanning neighborhoods.

Sources: https://www.globalsources.com/knowledge/chinese-fashion-trends-2024/ | https://jingdaily.com/posts/the-2024-china-core-fashion-forecast | https://radii.co/article/7-fashion-trends-to-look-for-in-china-in-2024

县城游 (County-level tourism):
- Definition: Young Chinese choosing small, non-famous county-level cities over major urban destinations. Drivers: overcrowding in 北上广深, lower cost, authenticity-seeking.
- Data: 2026 May Day holiday data on Zhihu showed small county towns outperformed major cities in net tourist satisfaction and growth rate.
- Examples: Dali (大理), Pingtan (平潭), Rongcheng (荣成), Dunhuang (敦煌), Yanji (延吉).
- For our site: Primarily a domestic Chinese phenomenon with limited direct impact on our 北上广深-focused site. However, it reinforces that our site should include a day-trips or beyond-the-city section for each of the four cities.

### F3. Trending module feasibility

A real-time trending module pulling live Xiaohongshu data is NOT feasible without a commercial API agreement with Xiaohongshu. The platform does not offer a public API.

Achievable alternatives:
- Curated local picks this season section: Manually updated monthly by monitoring Xiaohongshu, Douyin, and Zhihu — surfacing 3-5 trending spots per city with source: locals on 小红书 attribution and a link out.
- Semi-automated monitoring: Monitor a fixed set of Xiaohongshu search URLs for each city (stable even without login) and update content when trends shift.
- RSS-adjacent option: Mafengwo and Zhihu content is search-engine indexed; Google Alerts for city-specific Zhihu/Mafengwo URLs can catch freshly published 攻略 updates.

Recommended module design:
- Name: What Locals Are Doing This Month (本地人最近在玩)
- Content: 3 spots per city, refreshed monthly
- Method: Manual curation from Xiaohongshu visitor mode + Douyin same-city feed
- Attribution: Trending on 小红书 / Douyin with hyperlink to source note/video
- No scraping, no API, no reproduction — link-out only
